“Constraints on the active galactic nucleus and starburst activity of local ultraluminous infrared galaxies from a broad range of torus models”

Published in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society

Posted on February 21, 2025

I am pleased to share our research article “Constraints on the active galactic nucleus and starburst activity of local ultraluminous infrared galaxies from a broad range of torus models” in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society.

In this paper we further explore the properties of the HERschel Ultra Luminous nfrared Galaxy Survey (HERUS) sample of 42 local ultraluminous infrared galaxies (ULIRGs) with our recently developed Bayesian spectral energy distribution (SED) fitting code SMART (Spectral energy distributions Markov chain Analysis with Radiative Transfer models). We fit the SEDs of the HERUS ULIRGs with four different models for the active galactic nucleus (AGN) torus, a starburst and a spheroidal galaxy model, to put constraints on the AGN fraction of the galaxies and their star formation rate (SFR). Two of the AGN torus models we explored are smooth and two are two-phase. For more details, please read the article.
